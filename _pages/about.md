I'm a final-year student at IIT Bombay. I am pursuing an Interdisciplinary Dual Degree Program with a Bachelor's in Electrical Engineering and a Master's in Artificial Intelligence & Data Science.

I am interested in taking inspiration from the brain to create AI algorithms. My research interests include neuromorphic computing, spiking neural networks, self-supervised learning, and designing energy-efficient AI algorithms. I am also interested in AI hardware: hardware-aware ML, algorithm-hardware codesign, and in-memory computing.

# Research Experience

## Self‑Supervised Pre‑Training of Spiking Neural Networks | RWTH Aachen/FZ Julich
Guide: [Prof. Emre Neftci, Neuromorphic Software Ecosystems Lab](https://www.fz-juelich.de/en/pgi/pgi-15)
 - Self‑supervised contrastive learning: Developing a framework with joint training of an artificial neural network (ANN) and
spiking neural network (SNN) to assimilate (contrast) related (unrelated) frame‑event stream pairs
 - Conducting joint training of frame‑event stream pairs through contrastive learning, enhancing the SNN’s capabilities
 - Highlighting the effectiveness of pre‑training large‑scale SNNs using raw data from event cameras by demonstrating improvements on various downstream event‑based classification benchmarks

## Spiking Neural Network Algorithms | Master’s Thesis, IIT Bombay
Guide: [Prof. Udayan Ganguly, MeLoDe Lab](https://nanomemorylogic.wordpress.com/) | Head: SemiX, IITB
 - Implemented lottery ticket hypothesis, a pruning method, in recurrent spiking neural networks, achieving 85% sparsity
 - Demonstrated equivalence of a trained liquid state machine to a sparse recurrent spiking neural network
 - Implemented learnable delays between consecutive layers in an SNN for improved temporal data processing

## Enhanced Regularization in On‑Chip Learning | Bachelor’s Thesis, IIT Bombay
Guide: [Prof. Udayan Ganguly, MeLoDe Lab](https://nanomemorylogic.wordpress.com/) | Head: SemiX, IITB <br>
Awarded **Undergraduate Research Award 02** for one of the best bachelor’s thesis in the EE department

 - Implemented an analog & temporary on‑chip memory (ATOM) cell with controllable retention timescales for the weights
